What is EDM?

"EDM" stand for Electronic Dance Music and is music mostly played at clubs, raves, and music festivals.
EDM is characterized in general by a repetitive percussion track, often with a melody played by a synthesizer laid over percussion.
There are numerous sub-genres of EDM which is why not all EDM sounds the same. Below is a simple break down of a few sub-genres and the specific festivals held for these genres.

Sub-Genre Description Festival
House House music is a genre of electronic dance music that originated in Chicago in the early 1980s. It is characterized by a repetitive 4/4 beat and often features synthesized basslines, drum machines, and soulful vocals. Day Trip
Techno Techno is a genre of electronic dance music that emerged in Detroit in the mid-1980s. It is characterized by a repetitive, hypnotic beat and often features synthesized Skyline
Trance Trance is a genre of electronic dance music that originated in Germany in the early 1990s. It is characterized by a tempo of around 125-150 beats per minute, and often features melodic synthesizer lines and a build-up of tension leading to a climax. Dreamstate
Dubstep Dubstep is a genre of electronic dance music that originated in South London in the late 1990s. It is characterized by a tempo of around 140 beats per minute, and often features heavy basslines, syncopated rhythms, and a focus on sound design. Lost Lands
Hardstyle Hardstyle is a genre of electronic dance music that originated in the Netherlands in the early 2000s. It is characterized by a tempo of around 140-150 beats per minute, and often features distorted kick drums, heavy basslines, and a focus on melody and atmosphere. Wastelands
